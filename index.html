Who's this guy?

I work on many-body theories of cold atom and condensed matter physics. Recently, I have been particularly interested in highly non-equilibrium systems. It is a lot of fun to watch the two theoretical cornerstones — Landau's scheme of symmetry breaking and the theory of topological matters — receive major renovations when the extra dimension of time becomes essential. My research branch outs include spin liquids, large spin ultracold gases, and the effects of background manifold on quantum systems. (CV.pdf)
I will be joining Kavli Institute of Theoretical Sciences (KITS) at University of Chinese Academy of Science, Beijing, China, as an assistant professor in the fall of 2021.

    • Floquet time crystals without disorders
       
      Biao Huang, Ying-Hai Wu and W. Vincent Liu, “Clean Floquet Time Crystals: Models and Realizations in Cold Atoms”, Phys. Rev. Lett. 120, 110603 (2018)    arXiv:1703.04663
      "My solids does not have enough disorder to induce many-body localization. It's not starting from low temperature prethermal states either. Am I still in the game of searching for time crystals?" → "YES!"

  • Quantum spin liquids
    • Projective symmetry group classification of spin liquids
      Biao Huang, Yuan-Ming Lu and Yong Baek Kim, “Interplay of nonsymmorphic symmetry and spin-orbit coupling in hyperkagome spin liquids: Applications to Na4Ir3O8”, Phys. Rev. B 95, 054404 (2017)    arXiv:1610.06191
      "Hyperkagome" lattice has 12 sublattices per unit cell, but it only supports 3 kinds of Z2 spin liquids on it — much more manageable than the >100 types on square lattices!
      Also, this paper can be a good pedagogical material for learning PSG classification from scratch, a note for myself to recall how to do it, too....
